Charles Jean Mathieu Stroom        

Best seen with Firefox

Born in the great city of Maastricht, famous for its beauty and character, the "Treaty of Maastricht", the language "ut Mestreechs" and, of course, the yearly Carnival and "vastelaovend vereiniging de Tempeleers", but living in Amsterdam since long.

Both are actually great cities of the Netherlands, or Holland, its more familiar but incorrect name.

Formerly working at the European Space Research and Technology Centre, ESTEC in Noordwijk. ESTEC is the largest centre of the European Space Agency ESA. You will also find the Space Expo in Noordwijk.

Interests and hobbies are but a few, but nevertheless can take quite some time. I keep track of a couple of indexes to periodicals in these domains. They include:

  • Music, especially early music in authentic performances (Early Music, FoMRHI Quarterly, the Galpin Society Journal and the Dutch Bouwbrief of the Dutch association het Bouwerskontakt;
  • Art, with a preference for sculptures, and the Italian renaissance (FMR).
  • Woodworking on a small scale (Fine Woodworking, American Woodworker and since short, also Popular Woodworking).
  • Metalworking since a few years (Model Engineer Workshop).

All my indexes are maintained with grok (many thanks to Thomas Driemeyer), and they are now all in the same basic format. Unfortunately, grok seems no longer maintained by Thomas, so I keep my fingers crossed. From the basic 'grok' file format it is quite easy to generate CSV files (comma separated value files) and you can download these CSV files for use in your own spreadsheet from the download area *). (You may need to use shift-button_1 click on the file name to start the download, rather then to see the files.) Although the website use proper html code for accented characters, such as '&agrave;' for 'à', the CSV files are converted to the UTF-8 character set.

*) Unfortunately, the site on github does not show the list of csv files available in directory "downloads" as used to be the case. If you are desperate you still can download any specific csv file by specifying its name iin your browser as https://stremen.github.io/xxx_utf8.csv where "xxx" is the character name of the particular subject of interest (aww, bbb, emm, fmr, fomrhi, fww, galpin, mew or pww).
